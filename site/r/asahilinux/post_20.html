Charge threshold when powered off

by OkBobcat8622 | 10 points

I've read through a bunch of posts, but haven't seen any mention of charge thresholds when powered off (there are a couple outside Asahi). I have "Preserve Battery Health" enabled in the GNOME settings Power menu. When my macbook is turned on, I can see this feature works (doesn't charge >80%), but when the macbook is powered off, it seems to charge to 100%. So I guess the OS just controls the charge behaviour when it's turned on and doesn't communicate the threshold to the firmware. Is it possible to get the threshold working when powered off? Is macos able to do this?

Thank you!

Edit:

Just found a comment from Marcan:
https://www.reddit.com/r/AsahiLinux/comments/13g2h3b/comment/jk3odu1

Sounds like the firmware charge threshold doesn't work when the device is powered off:

Instead we are adding support for a hardcoded 80% mode which is managed by the firmware, which does not have a configurable threshold, but will work in s2idle and full sleep mode (when it comes)

And AlDente charge limiter (for macOS) says the same:

AlDente is typically unable to modify the charging behavior of a MacBook while it is in sleep mode or powered off [...] With the feature enabled and the MacBook powered off, the charge limit reset and will charge to 100%."

IIRC the current firmware only supports very specific charging thresholds. To let users set arbitrary thresholds, the OS has to enforce them externally – by starting and ending the charging process at the right time.
