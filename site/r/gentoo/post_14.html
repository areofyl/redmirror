Boot with a flash drive and take a look at your gentoo partition. There should be a vmlinuz file in /boot, if there's not you need to install your kernel. If it is there, then grub-mkconfig is not finding it automatically.

Yeah gentoo's grub script looks for a kernel with a specific name format like vmlinuz-<kernel version>-gentoo, if it is customized or just named "vmlinuz" the grub script in 10_linux may not find it, but you can add it manually to 40_custom

Btw, I looked at your old post about it, and if you'd like to have your gentoo and arch show above uefi on your grub menu, you can rename 40_custom to 15_custom and rerun grub-mkconfig. The numbering is just used to force the order; grub loads them in sorted order by filename :)
