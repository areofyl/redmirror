It only kinda is. Complete isolation is heavy on performance because of the need of a translation layer between the VM and actual hardware, that's why by default most virtualization software breaks isolation in specific cases (e.g. virtqueues in virtio-SCSI, the default storage driver in qemu). It's possible to configure stronger isolation but the business focus has been on performance and I doubt that they will suddenly do the opposite and go for complete isolation, especially with hardware prices.

I do hope for a shift towards more isolation though.
