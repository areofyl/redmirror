A Chrome renderer that looked like a 150 MB process was secretly holding 136 GB of RAM+swap on my Ubuntu box. Anyone seen this?

by Similar_Rich_1563 | 18 points

A weird pattern has been bothering me for a couple of weeks. Setup is Ubuntu 24.04, kernel 6.17, AMD RX 6700 XT (amdgpu), GNOME on Wayland, Chrome 147. 64 GB RAM + 128 GB swap.

The trigger is very specific: I have to leave the machine on, logged in, with the display still up, nobody touching it. Walk away for a few hours, come back, find earlyoom has been SIGTERM-ing Chrome tabs all night because memory and swap both got eaten. If I manually suspend before leaving, it does not happen — I come back to a healthy machine. So whatever's leaking only runs while the session is awake and idle. That already feels like a hint (some tab's requestAnimationFrame? a background video that keeps drawing frames?) but I can't prove which tab without better tooling.

Every 3–4 hours, Shmem in /proc/meminfo climbs from near-zero to ~25 GB at a steady ~600 MB/min, sits there for a couple hours, and then suddenly drops back to near-zero. Looks like this:

2026-04-21 22:10 Shmem = 23,698 MB ← growing for ~35 min

2026-04-22 00:01 Shmem = 61 MB ← collapse

2026-04-22 00:48 Shmem = 23,805 MB ← growing again

2026-04-22 03:37 Shmem = 13 MB ← collapse

2026-04-22 05:18 Shmem = 24,368 MB ← again

Swap also fills up to 115 GB around the peaks because the kernel tries to offload what won't fit in RAM.

The confusing part:

I wrote a little script that snapshots top-30 processes every 2 min (PSS / RSS / Shmem / Swap from /proc/PID/smaps_rollup). Every time I look at a peak snapshot, no process shows anywhere near 25 GB. The biggest Shmem numbers are Chrome renderers at ~120 MB each, a handful of them. Adding up the whole top-30 only gets me to ~1.3 GB. Where's the other 24 GB?

Then I caught an earlyoom kill. At 03:35:55:

Shmem (system) = 23,862 MB

MemAvailable = 2,607 MB

SwapFree = 13,173 MB (→ 115 GB swap used)

At 03:36:50 earlyoom sent SIGTERM to two Chrome renderers:

SIGTERM chrome PID 2052745 VmRSS 158 MiB

SIGTERM chrome PID 1345129 VmRSS 153 MiB

At 03:37:56 (2 min after peak, right after the kills):

Shmem (system) = 13 MB (-23,849 MB)

MemAvailable = 27,705 MB (+25,098 MB)

SwapFree = 124,680 MB (+111,507 MB!)

So killing two Chrome renderers whose /proc/smaps_rollup claimed ~120 MB of Shmem apiece (~240 MB combined) actually released 25 GB of RAM and 111 GB of swap. That's ~136 GB of shared memory held by two processes that looked innocent.

The other ~10 Chrome renderers stayed alive through the whole thing. Their Shmem numbers didn't change before or after. Actually, an hour earlier earlyoom had already killed 9 other Chrome renderers and system Shmem didn't budge — only those two PIDs mattered.

My guess: Chrome (or mutter, or something in between) is handing GPU buffers to the compositor via memfd_create or dma_buf, and those buffers get held open by an fd after the backing file is unlinked. Once that happens:

The pages count toward system-wide Shmem and can be swapped

But they don't appear as a mapping in the owning process's smaps

Only when the fd closes (= process dies) does the kernel reclaim them

If anyone knows whether that's actually how it works, I'd love to hear it. Because if so, there's no way for a normal user to tell "which tab is leaking GPU memory" — Chrome's own task manager doesn't show this either.

Anyone else on Wayland + amdgpu + Chrome seeing this? It's suspicious that it's always exactly two renderers. I suspect specific tabs but I don't know how to identify which.

I've got the raw files (earlyoom journal for the kill window, full-day memsnap log, the timeline CSV, a 4-panel chart, the logger scripts if you want to reproduce on your own box). Happy to share any of it — just ask and I'll drop a gist link.

Thanks in advance. This has been bugging me for weeks.
