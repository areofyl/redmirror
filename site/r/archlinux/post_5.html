BanaTibor 9

On unix like systems the stupid drives concept is not used. Your file systems are organized into one tree with one root. The root / is the starting point, any other file systems on another drives or on the network or on another partition is mounted into this tree. Historically the mount points are in /mnt, these are just directories. Use the "mount" command to list the attached filesystems and there mounting points.
